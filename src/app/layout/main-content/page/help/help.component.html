Chemical Screening
Users can upload a file with multiple molecules (at most 1000) stored in sdf or SMILES formats via “Chemical Screening” sub-menus and provide a valid e-mail address for receiving calculation results. Users can click the “Submit” button to deliver the query molecules to YaTCM Server. After retrieving the users’ job successfully, the calculation starts on the backend server of YaTCM. A typical run containing a molecule takes 2 ~ 3 min, while the run time will depend on the number of molecules.
FAQ

1. What is SEA?
In silico target prediction is critical for drug discovery and development, on which there is a substantial body of research (Ballester PJ, Frontiers in Chemistry, 4, 2016, 1-10). One such method—SEA—has proved to be a promising tool and has been successfully applied in drug repositioning (Keiser MJ, Nature, 462, 2009, 175-181; Keiser MJ, Nature Biotechnology, 25, 2007, 197-206; Cameron R.T. et al. Biochemical Pharmacology 85, 2013, 1297–1305) and natural products target prediction (Sá MS, Journal of Natural Products, 74, 2011, 2269-2272). In our previous work, we proposed a multi-voting SEA model for small molecule target prediction (Wang Z, Journal of Cheminformatics, 8, 2016, 20). By utilizing the multi-voting SEA model, all compounds in YaTCM can be systematically predicted and mapped to single protein targets from the ChEMBL database. Four different molecular fingerprints (MACCS, Morgan, Topological and Atom pair) were used to build SEA models. 2. What is KEGG metabolic pathway mapping?
The concept of metabolite-likeness, which is derived from the fact that drugs are more structurally similar to endogenous metabolites than library compounds, is a useful principle in drug design (Drug Discovery Today, Volume 14, Numbers 1/2, January 2009). Kim et al. (Kim HU, Nature Biotechnology, 33, 2015, 264-268) reported that the degree of structural similarity between compounds derived from traditional oriental medicine and human metabolites is even higher than that between small drug molecules and human metabolites. They argue that mapping of TCM compounds to human metabolic pathway based on structural similarity may be useful in analyses of mechanisms of action. Therefore, in YaTCM, all compounds were mapped to KEGG human metabolic pathway by calculating structural similarity of TCM-metabolite pairs. In addition, we further mapped therapeutic targets to KEGG human signal transduction pathways. To quantitatively elucidate the importance of these pathways, we further applied commonly used gene-ontology enrichment analysis (Nucleic Acids Research, 2009, Vol. 37, No. 1 1–13) to rank the score of these pathways. Figure 2 shows the pathway mapping result in prescription Yu Ping Feng. The corresponding compound and protein are outlined by a red circle and a blue rectangle, respectively. It is possible to click on these objects to acquire the corresponding relationship between YaTCM and KEGG. 3.what are Network building and analysis?
To visualize and graphically analyze the relationship of the different aspects of YaTCM data, we developed a visualization interface using ECharts and NetworkX, allowing the information of the interactive network to be displayed within a webpage. The network button on the result page can be used to render a ‘Force’ dynamic network graph or ‘Circle’ static network graph. Users can filter superabundant no-drug molecular nodes and links through setting four vital ADMET parameters and Tao’s drug-likeness parameter (W. Tao et al. Journal of Ethnopharmacology, 145, 2013, 1–10). The four ADMET parameters, include stars (default value is 0~4), QPlogBB (default is -3.0~1.2), RuleOfFive (default is 0~4), PercentHumanOralAbsorption (default is 0.0~100.0) (see Table S3 in Supporting information). The default value of drug-likeness parameter is > 0.18. Networks can be exported in Portable Network Graphic (PNG) format. 4.What is prescription explorer?
When treating diseases using TCM, a prescription or herbal formula containing various herbs is usually applied. To better understand the molecular mechanism of action of TCM prescription or herbal formulas, YaTCM can explore a TCM prescription or herbal formulas from a network and pathways perspective. Firstly, the common therapeutic targets (Figure A) and diseases (Figure C) between any two herbs are identified and rendered as an interactive network graph. Secondly, the herb-herb interacting pairs were constructed based on a certain number of common therapeutic targets, which are ether involved in the same KEGG pathway (Figure D) or share a certain number of diseases (Figure B). Clicking the edge between two nodes reveals three links, a list of common KEGG pathways (Figure D,2), a list of shared therapeutic targets (Figure D,1) and a list of therapeutic targets that appeared only in the common KEGG pathway (Figure D,3). 5.Have display problems?
